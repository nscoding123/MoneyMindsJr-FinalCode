Interest

When you put your money in your savings account and leave it there, the bank gives you a little extra money as a reward. This extra money is called "interest." It's like a thank-you for letting the bank keep your money safe.

Remember: Interest is calculated both while Saving and Borrowing. While saving, you are paid interest; but while borrowing you pay interest.

Interest - Types

There are two types of interest: Simple and Compound Interest.

Simple interest is like getting a small bonus on your money. Let's say you saved $10 in your bank, and the bank gives you 5% interest. That means the bank will add 5% of $10 to your money. 5% of $10 is 50 cents, so you'll have $10 + 50 cents = $10.50 after a year.

Now compund interest on the other hand is when your comney gets compunded over time. For example, you have $10 in your bank, and the bank gives you 5% compound interest. In the beginning, you'll get 5% of $10, just like with simple interest (50 cents). But here's the magic: the next time, the bank gives you 5% of $10.50, which is 53 cents. It's like interest on top of interest!

Did you know: Savings account rates are primarily impacted by Federal Reserve policy.
